About me Projects Tinker Cad Free Cad Fabricating Fusion Virtual Reality

For the final project of the class, I built a Parkour game! Unity was honestly so easy compared to everything else I endured in this class. It felt like an easy roblox game builder. More often than not I would just drag and drop. I decided to make a parkour game because of my love for roblox growing up. I would used to play this speed run parkour and it was always in first person, when I first started my project and I was just testing out walking and jumping, it immediately brought me back. I honestly did not have much issues, however I did have some hiccups. First, I couldn't get to walk or jump in the game. I easily fixed that by going over to my settings and enable both for active input. Next, I kept getting my objects pink. Like the previous issue, it was an easy fix just going over to settings and enabling my render pipeline. I then kept running into some user error just due to the novelty of me using the program, but I was able to solve all of them and get it going. I added two asset packs, first was a simple nature pack. This pack just provided some cool trees and rocks, nothing really crazy. The next was an animal pack. This pack was just to add some cool animals to the mix. I added a chicken, a cat, horse, and a moose. Overall this project was another enjoyable experience, I just wish I had more time to edit and make it actually challenging. To play the game, I got my girlfriend to give it a shot. There are two clips under one of her playing it shot on my phone, and the other simultaniously on the laptop recording. Unfortunately, I had to cut it just to show how the game is completed due to it being such a large file size
